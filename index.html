  1. Research Assistant

    Concordia University

    2023-01-01 — Present

    Knowledge-Informed Auto-Penetration Testing Based on Reinforcement Learning with Reward Machine

    • We proposed a knowledge-informed AutoPT framework (DRLRM-PT), which utilizes RMs to encode domain knowledge as guidelines to train PT policies.

    • We applied RMs to specify and arrange subtasks of PT based on existing PT practices. We also designed a dedicated event set for AutoPT , where events represent successful executions of adversary tactics defined in cybersecurity knowledge bases, which are used by RMs to assign subtasks to agents during PT.

    • We took the lateral movement of PT as a case study and formulated PT as a POMDP guided by RMs, where two RMs were designed based on MITRE ATT&CK knowledge base; DQRM algorithm was applied to optimize the PT policy. This case study proves the effectiveness of our proposed approach and provides an illustrative example of embedding existing cybersecurity knowledge bases into RL/DRL-based PT.
